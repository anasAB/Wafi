Read before touching a frame.Language & DirectionDesign every frame in Arabic RTL first. The English LTR version is a mirror — do not re-layout, just flip direction and swap copy.All UI labels use shop-owner language, never accounting language.Never writeAlways writeRevenue / Revenuesالدخل  (Income / what came in)Expenses / Expenditureالمصاريف  (What you spent)Net income / Net profitالربح  (What you made)Accounts receivableديون الزبائن  (What customers owe you)Inventory / Stock on handالمخزون  (Your stock)Transaction / Invoiceبيعة  (Sale)Void transactionإلغاء البيعة  (Cancel the sale)Apply discountخصم  (Discount)Subtotalالمجموع قبل الخصم  (Total before discount)Totalالمجموع  (Total)Tender amountالمبلغ المدفوع  (Amount paid)Change dueالباقي  (Change)Currency Display RulesTwo currencies always: USD ($) and Syrian Pound (SYP / ل.س).Display USD as primary — larger text. SYP as secondary — smaller text, lighter color, below or inline.Price format USD: $25.00  |  Price format SYP: 337,500 ل.سUse Western numerals (0-9) for all currency amounts — this is the regional convention for money even in Arabic UI.Use Eastern Arabic numerals (٠١٢٣٤٥٦٧٨٩) only for non-currency counts: quantity, item count, alert counts.Exchange rate is set manually by the owner. It is never auto-fetched in v1. The owner updates it every morning. Design must make this a 2-tap action from anywhere.SYP equivalent formula: SYP amount = USD amount × exchange rate. Always recalculates live when rate changes.Sync State — Permanent Top Bar IndicatorSyrian internet drops constantly. The sync state must always be visible. Rules for how to handle locked features:If a feature is not in the customer's active packs: HIDE it entirely. Do not grey it out. Do not show a lock icon. The UI must feel complete for what the customer has paid for.Exception: if the owner (not cashier) is in a settings or configuration screen, gated features may be shown with an "upgrade" prompt. On operational screens (POS, Dashboard) — hide completely.Customer Pack gates: credit payment option in POS, Customers tab in Dashboard nav.Staff Pack gates: cashier shift info in POS top bar, cashier activity card on Dashboard, Z-report access.Reporting Pack gates: Reports tab in Dashboard nav, WhatsApp digest configuration.Electronics Pro gates: IMEI field on cart item, repair ticket workflow. Usually an employee, not the owner. May have low tech literacy.Device: Phone (primary design target). Tablet in landscape (secondary — described separately at end of this section).Session: The cashier opens the POS at the start of their shift. The app stays open all day on the counter. They never navigate away to another app or screen during a sale.Physical setup: Phone or tablet on a counter stand. USB barcode scanner plugged in. Thermal printer connected. Customer standing on the other side of the counter.One-handed use: The cashier's right hand holds the phone or operates the counter-mounted device. Left hand may be handling products.Internet: May be offline for hours at a time. The POS must work identically offline. No feature degrades.Screen Layout — Permanent StructureThe POS screen has three permanent zones that never change regardless of state:Zone A — Top Bar (permanent, never hides)→ Height: 56px fixed.→ Background: White with a 1px bottom border (#E5E7EB).→ LEFT side (RTL — appears on right visually): Shop name in 14px medium weight. Below it in 11px regular: cashier name + shift duration if Staff Pack is active (e.g., "أحمد · ٢س ١٥د"). If Staff Pack is inactive: show only cashier name.→ CENTER: Sync state indicator (dot + text as defined in global rules).→ RIGHT side (RTL — appears on left visually): Three-dot menu icon (24px). Tapping opens a bottom sheet with: "تبديل الكاشير" (switch cashier), "إغلاق الوردية" (close shift — Staff Pack only), "إعدادات الطابعة" (printer settings), "تسجيل الخروج" (log out).Zone B — Product Discovery (top ~55% of screen)→ This is where the cashier finds products to add to the cart.→ Contains: search bar at the top, category tabs below it, product grid below that.→ Scrollable vertically. The search bar stays sticky at the top of this zone.Zone C — Cart + Checkout (bottom ~45% of screen)→ This is where the cashier sees the current sale and takes payment.→ In State 1 (empty cart): this zone shows an empty state illustration.→ In States 2–4 (active cart, payment, receipt): this zone is occupied by the cart, payment screen, or receipt.→ The checkout button lives at the very bottom of this zone — always reachable by thumb.⚡ On small phones (5" screen, ~360px wide): Zone B collapses to show only the search bar + category tabs. The product grid is hidden until the cashier searches or taps a category. This prevents overflow.STATE 1  Idle — Empty CartCashier is waiting for a customer. Nothing in the cart.Zone B content in State 1Search Bar→ Full width, 48px tall, rounded corners (8px radius).→ Placeholder text (Arabic): "ابحث أو امسح الباركود…"→ Left icon (RTL — appears on right): magnifying glass icon (20px), grey (#9CA3AF).→ Right icon (RTL — appears on left): barcode icon (20px), grey. Tapping activates camera barcode scanner as fallback if no USB scanner.→ The search bar is ALWAYS focused and listening for barcode scanner input. The USB barcode scanner sends keyboard events — the search bar must capture them without the cashier tapping the field. This is critical.→ When typing begins: product results appear instantly below as a list (not the grid). Results update on every keystroke.→ When a barcode is scanned via USB scanner: the barcode string populates the field for ~300ms (visible to cashier as confirmation), then the matching product is found and added to the cart automatically. The field clears and returns to placeholder. If barcode is not found: field stays populated with the scanned code and a "not found" state appears.Category Tabs→ Horizontal scrolling row of pill-shaped tabs. Height: 36px.→ First tab always: "الكل" (All). Followed by shop's configured categories (e.g., "إلكترونيات", "إكسسوار", "شواحن").→ Active tab: filled background (#1A56DB), white text. Inactive: white background, grey text (#4B5563), grey border.→ Tapping a tab filters the product grid to that category. "الكل" shows all products.→ Categories are configured in Back Office. If the shop has not set up categories, show only "الكل".Product Grid→ 2-column grid on phone. 3-column on tablet.→ Each product card: white background, 1px border (#E5E7EB), 8px radius.→ Product card contents top to bottom: product image (square, 1:1 ratio, fills card width — if no image: grey placeholder with product initial letter), product name in Arabic (2 lines max, truncate with ellipsis), price in USD (bold, 15px), price in SYP (smaller, 12px, grey).→ If stock = 0: overlay the card with a semi-transparent grey layer and a "نفد" (out of stock) badge in the top corner. Card is still tappable — allow adding to cart with a warning.→ If stock ≤ low-stock threshold (set in Back Office, default 5): show a small amber dot in the top corner. No other change.→ Tapping a product card with no variants: immediately adds 1 unit to the cart. A brief scale animation (card pops slightly) confirms the add. No confirmation modal.→ Tapping a product card WITH variants (e.g., color, size): opens a Variant Picker bottom sheet (described below).Variant Picker Bottom Sheet→ Slides up from the bottom. Background: white. Top handle bar (32px wide, 4px tall, grey, centered).→ Product image at the top (if exists). Product name below it.→ Variant groups listed vertically. Each group has a label ("اللون", "الحجم") and a horizontal row of selectable chips.→ Chip: 36px tall, rounded, border. Selected chip: brand color background, white text. Unselected: white, grey border.→ If a variant combination has zero stock: chip is shown with strikethrough text and 40% opacity. Still selectable but shows a "نفد" warning.→ Price may differ by variant — update the displayed price live as the cashier selects.→ Bottom of sheet: "إضافة للسلة" (Add to cart) button, full width, 48px tall. Disabled until all variant groups have a selection.Zone C content in State 1 — Empty Cart→ Light grey background (#F9FAFB).→ Center of the zone: a simple illustration (not a photograph — an icon-style drawing of a shopping bag or barcode scanner).→ Below illustration: "امسح الباركود أو اختر منتج" in 15px regular, grey (#6B7280).→ No checkout button. No other elements.→ Open cash drawer button: small, text-only button at very bottom of Zone C. "فتح الصندوق" with a cash drawer icon. Only visible if a printer (which triggers the drawer) is connected. Tapping triggers ESC/POS cash drawer open command.STATE 2  Active Cart — Items in CartOne or more items have been added. Building the order.Zone B in State 2Zone B stays identical to State 1. The cashier can keep searching and scanning to add more items. No change to Zone B.Zone C in State 2 — Cart→ Zone C is now occupied by the cart panel. White background.→ Top of Zone C: a thin drag handle (the cashier can drag this zone upward to expand the cart and collapse the product grid — this is a "half sheet" pattern). This lets the cashier review the full cart without losing access to product search.Cart Header→ One line: "السلة" (Cart) on the right (RTL), and the item count on the left: "٣ منتجات" (3 items). Item count uses Eastern Arabic numerals.Cart Line Items→ Each line item is one row. Height: 64px minimum.→ Row contents left to right (RTL — reversed visually):→   Right side: product name in Arabic (bold, 14px). Below name: unit price in USD (13px, grey). If a per-item discount is applied: show original price with strikethrough, then discounted price.→   Left side: quantity control — [ − ] [ ٢ ] [ + ] where the middle shows the quantity. Each button is 36×36px. The quantity number is tappable: tapping it opens a numeric keypad to type the quantity directly (faster for bulk, e.g., a customer buying 12 phone cases).→   Far left (RTL): line total in USD (bold, 14px). Below: line total in SYP (12px, grey).→   Delete row: swipe left on a cart line to reveal a red trash button. Or long-press to reveal delete. No dedicated delete icon on the row face — keeps the row clean.→ Rows are separated by a 1px divider (#E5E7EB).Cart Footer — below all line items→ Subtotal row: "المجموع" on the right, amount in USD on the left. Grey text (#4B5563).→ Discount row: only visible if a discount is applied. "خصم" on the right (in amber/orange to draw attention), discount amount in USD on the left with a minus sign. Example: "خصم  −$5.00"→ Total row: "الإجمالي" on the right (bold, 16px), total in USD on the left (bold, 16px). Below the USD total: SYP equivalent in 13px grey. Example: "ما يعادل ١,٣٥٠,٠٠٠ ل.س"→ Discount button: below the total rows. Text: "إضافة خصم" with a percentage icon. Tapping opens a Discount Bottom Sheet (described below).→ Add note button: below discount button. Text: "إضافة ملاحظة" with a pencil icon. Tapping opens a single-line text input. The note appears on the printed receipt.Discount Bottom Sheet→ Slides up. White background.→ Title: "الخصم" (Discount).→ Two tabs: "نسبة مئوية" (Percentage) and "مبلغ ثابت" (Fixed amount).→ Percentage tab: a large number input showing "٠" with a "%" suffix. Numeric keypad below it. Max value: 100.→ Fixed amount tab: a large number input with currency toggle — cashier taps to switch between USD and SYP. The other currency shows the equivalent live below.→ Below input: live preview — "خصم $5.00 على مجموع $25.00 = المجموع الجديد $20.00"→ The discount applies to the ENTIRE cart, not per item. Per-item discount is not in v1.→ Confirm button: "تطبيق الخصم". Cancels with "X" at top.→ To remove an existing discount: the discount row in the cart footer has a small "×" icon on its right side.Checkout Button→ Fixed at the very bottom of Zone C. Full width. Height: 56px.→ Background: brand blue (#1A56DB). Text: "الدفع  $25.00" — the word Checkout + the total amount. White text, 17px medium.→ As items are added or removed, the amount on the button updates live.→ If cart is empty: button shows "الدفع" with no amount and is disabled (60% opacity, not tappable).STATE 3  Payment ScreenCashier tapped Checkout. Customer is paying.The payment screen replaces Zone C entirely. Zone B (product search) is still visible but dimmed — the cashier cannot add items mid-payment. A back button in the payment header returns to the cart.Payment Screen Layout — top to bottomPayment Header→ One row: back arrow on the right (RTL) labeled "العودة للسلة", title "الدفع" in center.Amount Due Banner→ Large banner, brand blue background (#EBF1FD border, white fill alternative — designer's call on exact treatment).→ Center: "المبلغ المطلوب" label (13px, grey). Below it: the total in USD in 36px bold. Below that: SYP equivalent in 16px grey.→ Example: المبلغ المطلوب / $25.00 / ما يعادل ٣٣٧,٥٠٠ ل.سPayment Method Selection→ Label: "طريقة الدفع" (Payment method).→ Three large tiles in a row (or 2+1 if space is tight on small phones):→   Tile 1: "نقد دولار" (Cash USD) — dollar bill icon.→   Tile 2: "نقد ليرة" (Cash SYP) — Syrian pound icon or ل.س symbol.→   Tile 3: "بطاقة" (Card) — credit card icon.→   Tile 4 (Customer Pack only, hidden if pack inactive): "على الحساب" (Credit account) — person icon.→ Selected tile: brand blue border (2px), light blue background (#EBF1FD), blue icon and text. Unselected: grey border, white background.→ Default selection: "نقد دولار" is pre-selected when the payment screen opens.→ Split payment: a "+ إضافة طريقة دفع" link below the tiles. Tapping adds a second payment row (described in Split Payment section below).Cash USD Flow (selected state)→ Input field appears below the tiles, full width, large (56px tall).→ Label: "المبلغ المدفوع بالدولار".→ Numeric keypad appears (device native keypad, decimal allowed).→ Placeholder: the exact amount due — so if the customer pays exactly, the cashier just confirms without typing.→ As the cashier types the tendered amount, below the input appears live:→   "الباقي: $3.50" in green (16px bold) if change is due.→   "ناقص: $2.00" in red (16px bold) if tendered amount is less than total.→   "بالضبط ✓" in green if exactly matched.→ SYP equivalent of the change is shown below in 13px grey: "ما يعادل ٤٧,٢٥٠ ل.س"Cash SYP Flow (selected state)→ Same input pattern as Cash USD but the field accepts SYP amounts.→ Label: "المبلغ المدفوع بالليرة".→ Live conversion: below the SYP input, show the USD equivalent being tendered. Example: "يعادل $22.50"→ Change shown in SYP. Also show USD equivalent of change.Card Flow (selected state)→ No input field. No terminal integration in v1.→ Show a simple info line: "سيتم تسجيل الدفع ببطاقة بمبلغ $25.00"→ No amount entry needed — card pays the exact total.→ The confirm button becomes active immediately when card is selected.Credit Account Flow — Customer Pack only→ When "على الحساب" is selected, a customer search field appears.→ Label: "اختر الزبون" (Select customer).→ Search input: cashier types customer name or phone number. Results appear as a list below — each result shows: customer name (bold) and current credit balance in USD (e.g., "رصيد: $120.00").→ Selecting a customer confirms the link. Show selected customer name in a green chip with an "×" to change.→ If customer's existing credit balance + this sale would exceed their credit limit (configured in Back Office): show amber warning "رصيد الزبون سيتجاوز الحد المسموح ($200.00). متأكد؟" with Confirm and Cancel buttons.→ If the customer has no configured credit limit: allow without warning.→ The confirm button is labeled "تسجيل على الحساب" instead of "تأكيد الدفع".Split Payment→ When "+ إضافة طريقة دفع" is tapped, a second row appears below the first.→ First row: shows the first payment method with an amount input — cashier enters how much is paid by this method.→ Second row: a second payment method selector (same tiles) + amount input.→ Live reconciliation: below both rows, show "المدفوع: $20.00 + $5.00 = $25.00 ✓" when they sum to the total. Show "ناقص: $3.00" if they don't add up yet.→ Maximum 2 payment methods in v1. No third split.→ The confirm button stays disabled until the split amounts sum exactly to the total.Confirm Payment Button→ Full width, 56px tall. Fixed at the very bottom.→ Label: "تأكيد الدفع" (Confirm payment).→ Green background (#059669) when active. Grey when disabled.→ Disabled conditions: no payment method selected, tendered amount less than total (cash flows), split amounts don't sum to total.→ On tap: brief loading state (spinner in button, 300-800ms while the sale is being written to local DB and queued for sync). Then transition to State 4.STATE 4  Sale Complete — ReceiptPayment confirmed. Sale is closed. Receipt options shown.Receipt Screen Layout→ Full screen takeover — replaces both Zone B and Zone C.→ Background: white.Success confirmation→ Top area: a large checkmark animation. Circle draws itself in green over 400ms, checkmark appears inside.→ Below animation: "تمت البيعة" (Sale complete) in 22px medium.→ Below that: sale summary — total paid in USD (bold, 18px), number of items (13px grey). Example: "$25.00 · ٣ منتجات"→ If payment was split: show each method. Example: "$20 نقد دولار + $5 بطاقة"→ If change was given: "الباقي المُعطى: $3.50" in 13px grey.Receipt action buttons→ Two equal-width buttons side by side, 48px tall each.→ Button 1: "طباعة" (Print) — printer icon. On tap: sends ESC/POS command to connected printer. If print succeeds: brief "✓ تمت الطباعة" text under the button. If printer not connected or error: "فشلت الطباعة، حاول مجدداً" in red under button.→ Button 2: "واتساب" (WhatsApp) — WhatsApp icon (green). On tap: opens device share sheet (or WhatsApp directly) with pre-composed message containing sale total, itemized list, and shop name. No API integration — native device share.→ Below both buttons: "تخطي" (Skip) — small text link, grey, centered. Tapping immediately starts the auto-return countdown.Auto-return→ After 5 seconds from the success state appearing (whether or not the cashier interacts with receipt options), the screen automatically returns to State 1 (Idle).→ During the 5-second window: a thin progress bar at the top of the screen depletes left to right. This is subtle — it signals the countdown without alarming the cashier.→ If the cashier taps Print or WhatsApp: the 5-second timer resets to 5 seconds after their action completes.→ If the cashier taps Skip: immediate return to State 1.Returns Workflow — Accessible from State 1The cashier initiates a return from the idle state. A return is NOT a new state — it is a mode that modifies how the POS behaves.→ Entry point: in the three-dot menu in the top bar, one option is "استرجاع مبيعة" (Return a sale).→ Tapping "استرجاع مبيعة" opens a bottom sheet: search field labeled "رقم الفاتورة أو اسم الزبون" (receipt number or customer name). Results show past sales as rows: date, total, item count.→ Selecting a past sale: the POS loads that sale's items into a "return cart" — visually distinct from a regular cart. Background of Zone C changes to a light amber (#FFFBEB) to signal return mode. A "وضع الاسترجاع" (Return mode) banner appears at the top of Zone C.→ In the return cart: each item shows with a checkbox. The cashier unchecks items that are NOT being returned. Only checked items are returned.→ Each returned item quantity is editable — if the customer bought 3 but is returning 1, the cashier sets quantity to 1.→ Return total shown at the bottom: "المبلغ المُسترجع: $10.00".→ Return payment options: "نقد" (cash refund) or "رصيد على الحساب" (store credit). If Customer Pack is inactive: cash only.→ Confirm return button: "تأكيد الاسترجاع" — red background (#DC2626) to distinguish from a regular sale.→ After confirm: stock is added back automatically. A return receipt is generated (same format as sale receipt). Screen returns to State 1.Tablet Layout — Landscape OrientationOn tablet in landscape (768px+ wide), the POS uses a two-column layout. The owner may be at home, in a car, or at another location.Device: Phone ONLY. Portrait ONLY. This screen is never designed for tablet or desktop.Session: Owner opens this app multiple times per day — quick checks. Average session is under 2 minutes.Goal of each visit: Answer "am I making money, is anything wrong, what is happening at my shop" without navigating anywhere.Interaction: Mostly read-only. Two write actions exist: update exchange rate, capture expense.Offline: Shows last-synced data. Displays "آخر تحديث منذ X دقيقة" timestamp. Owner cannot make the app re-sync — it syncs automatically.Full Screen Layout — Top to Bottom in Exact OrderDesign the screen in this exact vertical order. No reordering. The profit number must be visible without scrolling on a 5" phone.Element 1 — App Header→ Height: 64px.→ Background: white.→ RIGHT side (RTL): Two lines of text. Line 1: time-aware greeting — "صباح الخير" (before 12:00), "مساء الخير" (12:00 onwards). 13px regular, grey (#6B7280). Line 2: Shop name. 18px medium, dark (#111928).→ LEFT side (RTL): Bell icon (24px). If there are unread alerts: a red dot (8px) overlaps the top-right corner of the bell icon. No number badge — just the dot.→ Tapping the bell: opens an Alerts History bottom sheet (see Element 5 for what counts as an alert). The sheet shows the last 30 days of alerts, newest first.
